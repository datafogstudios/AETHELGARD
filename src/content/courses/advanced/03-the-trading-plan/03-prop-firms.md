## The Illusion of Capital

Proprietary Trading Firms (Prop Firms) have revolutionized the industry by offering traders access to massive capital ($100k, $200k, etc.) for a small upfront fee. 

However, over 90% of traders fail these evaluations. Why? Because they misunderstand the math.

If you buy a $100,000 challenge, your account balance says $100,000. **This is a lie.** 

Prop firms enforce a strict Maximum Drawdown (usually 10% or $10,000) and a strict Daily Drawdown (usually 5% or $5,000). 
**Your true account size is $10,000.** If your equity dips by $10,000, you lose the account. Therefore, calculating risk based on $100,000 is the fastest way to fail.

## The Flaw of the 1% Rule

Standard retail advice says: "Risk 1% of your account per trade."

On a personal account, risking 1% is completely fine. If you hit a 5-trade losing streak, you are down 5%. You survive.

On a Prop Firm evaluation with a 5% Daily Drawdown limit, if you risk 1% (based on the $100k balance, which is $1,000), a 5-trade losing streak will hit $5,000 exactly. **You blow the account.** A 5-trade losing streak is a mathematical certainty for any strategy over a long enough timeline.

## Adapting Your Risk Model

To pass an evaluation, you must adjust your risk to ensure that a normal sequence of losses does not trigger the drawdown rules.

### The 0.25% to 0.5% Rule
Never risk more than **0.25% to 0.5%** of the total account balance per trade.
*   If the account is $100,000, risk $250 to $500 per trade.
*   By risking 0.25%, you would need to lose **20 trades in a row** in a single day to hit the 5% daily drawdown limit. 
*   If your edge is valid, losing 20 trades in a row is mathematically nearly impossible. You have effectively guaranteed your survival.

## The Strategy: Slow and Steady

Prop firms are a test of patience. The goal is to reach an 8% profit target on Phase 1.

If you risk 0.5% per trade, and your strategy yields an average 1:2 Risk/Reward ratio, every winner brings in 1%. To hit 8%, you simply need a net of 8 winning trades over the course of a month. 

### Do Not Rush
*   Many prop firms used to have a 30-day time limit. Most legitimate firms have removed this limit. You have infinite time. **Take it.**
*   If the market is choppy, do not trade. You are not forced to make money today. Your only job is to protect the equity.

### Dealing with Drawdown
If you hit a losing streak and are down 2% or 3% on the account, human psychology triggers panic. The amateur will double their risk to "make it back quickly." This always results in a blown account.

**The Professional Response to Drawdown:**
If your account dips into drawdown, you must immediately **cut your risk in half**. If you were risking 0.5%, drop to 0.25%. Your primary goal is no longer passing the challenge; your primary goal is clawing back to breakeven slowly and safely. 

## The Evaluation Psychology

The prop firm business model relies heavily on traders blowing accounts due to lack of emotional control. 
They want you to over-leverage. They want you to revenge trade.

When you pass a challenge, it is rarely because you found a magical technical setup. It is because you possessed the discipline to trade small, accept losses without anger, and execute your mechanical edge relentlessly over weeks without changing your rules.

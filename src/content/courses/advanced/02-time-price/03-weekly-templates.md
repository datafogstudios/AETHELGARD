## Expanding the Power of 3

In the previous lesson, we discussed how a single day is engineered using the Power of 3 (Accumulation, Manipulation, Distribution). 

This exact same algorithmic fractal occurs on the **Weekly Timeframe**. 

A single Weekly Candlestick has an Open, High, Low, and Close. Understanding the rhythm of the trading week allows you to anticipate *which specific days* are the highest probability days to execute trades.

## The Rhythm of the Week

### Monday: The Trap (Accumulation)
When the market opens on Sunday night (NY Time) / early Monday morning, the algorithm is resetting. Monday's typical profile is consolidation. It is creating a fake trend, or simply chopping sideways. **Monday's job is to trap retail traders into taking positions.**

If you are a swing trader, you generally avoid executing on a Monday. You are waiting for the algorithm to reveal its true manipulation leg.

### Tuesday / Wednesday: The Judas Swing (Manipulation)
This is where the magic happens. On Tuesday or Wednesday, the algorithm will aggressively manipulate price in the *opposite* direction of the true weekly trend. 

If the week is destined to be Bullish, Tuesday or Wednesday will feature a sharp drop *below* the Weekly Open price. This drop triggers the sell-stops of early buyers, and traps breakout sellers. 

This manipulation forms the **Low of the Week** (in a bullish template) or the **High of the Week** (in a bearish template).

### Thursday: The Expansion (Distribution)
Once the absolute high/low of the week has been locked in on Tuesday or Wednesday, Thursday provides the cleanest, fastest, and most one-sided expansion of the week. Thursday is the hallmark trend day. The algorithm is now rapidly delivering price to its ultimate weekly objective.

### Friday: The Close
Friday morning can offer a continuation of Thursday's trend. However, by mid-day (New York session), the major institutions begin closing their books for the week. This profit-taking results in price retracing back *against* the weekly trend, moving toward the Weekly Close.

## The Classic Bullish Weekly Template

Let's assume the Daily/Weekly charts indicate we are in a massive uptrend, reaching for a liquidity pool 300 pips away.

1. **The Weekly Open:** Mark the Sunday 5:00 PM NY Open price with a horizontal line on your chart.
2. **Monday:** Price chops around the opening price. Stay out.
3. **Tuesday:** During the London or NY Killzone, price suddenly drops sharply *below* the Weekly Open line. It hits a Daily Bullish Order Block or FVG. You see a 15-minute Market Structure Shift to the upside. 
4. **Execution:** You enter Long. You have just bought the absolute Low of the Week.
5. **Wednesday/Thursday:** Price continuously expands upwards, leaving behind FVGs and respecting them perfectly.
6. **Friday:** Price hits the 300-pip HTF objective, and begins to retrace downwards into the weekend.

## The Classic Bearish Weekly Template

Assume the HTF is strongly Bearish.

1. **The Weekly Open:** Mark the Opening price.
2. **Monday:** Accumulation.
3. **Tuesday/Wednesday:** Price manipulates sharply *upward*, trading *above* the Weekly Open into a massive premium area (like a 4H Bearish Breaker). This forms the High of the Week.
4. **Execution:** You enter Short above the Weekly Open.
5. **Thursday:** Massive downward expansion.
6. **Friday:** Target hit, price retraces upward into the close.

## Why Do We Use Templates?

Templates prevent you from over-trading.

Suppose it is Thursday. You wake up and look at the chart. The trend is bullish. However, you notice that the *Low of the Week* was fully established on Tuesday, and price is currently sitting 150 pips *above* the Weekly Open line.

If you try to "Buy the dip" on Thursday, you might succeed for a quick scalp, but you are buying at a massive weekly premium. Your risk is exponentially higher than the trader who bought the manipulation below the Weekly Open on Tuesday.

Understanding the weekly cycle allows you to know when to be aggressive (Tuesday/Wednesday after a sweep) and when to be defensive (Thursday/Friday chasing a move).

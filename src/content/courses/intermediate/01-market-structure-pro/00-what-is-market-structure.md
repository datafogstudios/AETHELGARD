## The Foundation of Price Action

Before we can use advanced supply and demand zones or liquidity concepts, we must understand the core language of the market: **Market Structure**. 

Market structure refers to the sequence of peaks (highs) and troughs (lows) that price creates as it moves. By reading this structure, you can determine who is in control of the market—buyers or sellers—and establish the dominant trend direction.

## Uptrend: Higher Highs and Higher Lows

In a healthy bullish market (uptrend), buyers are aggressively pushing the price up. 

- **Higher High (HH):** The peak of the upward swing is higher than the previous peak.
- **Higher Low (HL):** The pullback (trough) does not retrace below the previous trough before buyers step back in.

As long as the market continues to print a sequence of HHs and HLs, the trend is considered bullish. You should generally be looking for buying opportunities near the Higher Lows.

## Downtrend: Lower Highs and Lower Lows

Conversely, in a bearish market (downtrend), sellers are in control.

- **Lower Low (LL):** The trough of the downward swing is lower than the previous trough.
- **Lower High (LH):** The pullback (peak) does not break above the prior peak before sellers step back in and push price down again.

If you see a sequence of LLs and LHs, the trend is bearish. Your focus should be on finding selling opportunities near the Lower Highs.

## Structural Shifts (Market Structure Shift)

How do you know when a trend is ending? By looking for a break in the structural sequence.

If an uptrend fails to make a new Higher High, and instead breaks below the previous Higher Low to form a **Lower Low**, the bullish structure is broken. We call this a Market Structure Shift (MSS) or Break of Structure (BOS). This is your earliest structural clue that the trend may be reversing.

> ◈ **PRO INSIGHT:** Always analyze market structure on your higher timeframe (e.g., 4H or Daily) to establish your bias, and use your lower timeframe (e.g., 15m) to find corresponding structural entries.

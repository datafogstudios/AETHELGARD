## The Two Forces of the Market

Every single pip of movement you see on a price chart is the direct result of a tug-of-war between two opposing forces. In the financial world, these forces are famously named after two animals: **Bulls** and **Bears**.

### The Bulls (Buyers)

A **Bull** is an investor or trader who believes that a market, a specific currency, or an asset will **go up** in value.

- **The Action:** Bulls *Buy* (or go "Long").
- **The Attack:** They are called Bulls because of how a bull attacks its prey—by thrusting its horns **upward** into the air.
- **Market Impact:** When the Bulls are in control, Demand exceeds Supply. Because there are more willing buyers than sellers, sellers can demand a higher price. The market price rises. This is called a **Bull Market**.

### The Bears (Sellers)

A **Bear** is an investor or trader who believes that a market, a specific currency, or an asset will **go down** in value.

- **The Action:** Bears *Sell* (or go "Short").
- **The Attack:** They are called Bears because of how a bear attacks its prey—by swiping its paws **downward**.
- **Market Impact:** When the Bears are in control, Supply exceeds Demand. Because there are more willing sellers than buyers, sellers must lower their prices to attract buyers. The market price falls. This is called a **Bear Market**.

## It's All Relative (Forex Pairs)

In the stock market, being bullish means you think Apple stock will go up. 

In Forex, because you are trading *pairs*, the concept is slightly different. You can't just be "bullish on Forex." You have to be bullish on one currency *relative* to another.

If you are **Bullish on EUR/USD**:
- You believe the Euro will gain strength.
- OR, you believe the US Dollar will lose strength.
- Result: You expect the EUR/USD chart to go UP. You will click **Buy**.

If you are **Bearish on EUR/USD**:
- You believe the Euro will lose strength.
- OR, you believe the US Dollar will gain strength.
- Result: You expect the EUR/USD chart to go DOWN. You will click **Sell**.

### Visualizing the War

Open a chart and just watch the current price action. Every green candle represents a battle won by the Bulls. Every red candle represents a battle won by the Bears.

## Floors and Ceilings

Imagine an apartment building. You are throwing a bouncy ball. If you throw it down, it hits the floor and bounces up. If you throw it up, it hits the ceiling and bounces down.

In the Forex market, price acts exactly like that ball. 

**Support** is the floor. It is an area on the chart where price has dropped to before, but buyers stepped in aggressively to prevent it from going any lower.

**Resistance** is the ceiling. It is an area on the chart where price has risen to before, but sellers stepped in aggressively to prevent it from going any higher.

### How to Draw Zones

One of the biggest mistakes beginners make is drawing a thin, single line on their chart and expecting price to respect it to the exact pip. 

Institutions don't trade on exact single numbers. They build huge orders in *areas*. Therefore, you should draw your Support and Resistance as Rectangular Zones.

1. **Find the touches:** Look back in time. Where did price cleanly bounce at least twice?
2. **Draw a rectangle:** Cover the area from the wicks to the candlestick bodies. 

## The Psychology of S&R

Why do these levels work? It all boils down to human psychology: **Memory and Pain.**

Imagine you bought EUR/USD at 1.1000. It suddenly shoots up to 1.1200. You made great money! What are you going to do if it drops back down to 1.1000 a week later? You will probably try to buy it again to repeat the success.

Now imagine you didn't buy at 1.1000. You missed it. You feel pain and FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out). If price gives you a second chance and drops back to 1.1000, you will aggressively buy so you don't miss out again.

Because millions of traders across the globe are staring at the exact same charts, these historical pivot points become self-fulfilling prophecies.

## The Principle of Polarity

What happens when a bouncy ball is thrown down so hard that it breaks through the floor? It goes down into the room below. Now, if the ball bounces up, that old floor has become the new ceiling.

In trading, this is called the **Principle of Polarity**.

- **When Support breaks**, it frequently becomes **New Resistance**.
- **When Resistance breaks**, it frequently becomes **New Support**.

This is one of the most reliable and highly traded patterns in all of financial markets, often referred to as a "Break and Retest".

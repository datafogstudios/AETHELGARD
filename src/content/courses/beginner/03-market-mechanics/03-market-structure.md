## The Trend is Your Friend

There is an old saying on Wall Street: *"The trend is your friend until the end when it bends."*

Market Structure is the most reliable way to determine what the trend is, when it is continuing, and when it is ending. The market does not move in straight lines. It moves in zig-zags (waves).

By tracking the peaks and valleys of these waves, we can map the structure of the market.

## 1. The Uptrend (Bullish Structure)

An uptrend occurs when the buyers are aggressively pushing the market higher.

However, price never goes straight up. After a big push, buyers take profit, and sellers step in, causing a temporary pullback (a dip). Then, buyers re-enter, and push the price even higher than before.

An uptrend is formally defined by a series of:
- **Higher Highs (HH):** Every new peak is higher than the previous peak.
- **Higher Lows (HL):** Every new valley (pullback) is higher than the previous valley.

As long as the price continues to make Higher Lows, the uptrend is intact.

## 2. The Downtrend (Bearish Structure)

A downtrend occurs when sellers are in total control.

After a big drop, the market will temporarily rally upwards (a pullback). Then, sellers aggressively re-enter and smash the price down to a new low.

A downtrend is formally defined by a series of:
- **Lower Highs (LH):** Every new peak (pullback) is lower than the previous peak.
- **Lower Lows (LL):** Every new valley is lower than the previous valley.

As long as the price continues to make Lower Highs, the downtrend is intact.

## 3. Consolidation (The Sideways Chop)

Markets only trend about 30% of the time. The other 70% of the time, the market is resting, accumulating orders, or simply confused. This is known as **Consolidation** or a **Ranging Market**.

In consolidation, the market stops making clear Higher Highs or Lower Lows. Instead, price bounces sideways between a horizontal ceiling and a horizontal floor. 

*Beginner traps are often set in consolidation zones.* It is highly recommended to wait for the market to visibly "break out" of consolidation and establish a clear trend before trading.

## Break of Structure (BOS)

How do you know when a trend is over? You look for a **Break of Structure**.

If the market is in a Downtrend (making Lower Highs and Lower Lows), the bears are in control. Suddenly, the market pushes up and breaks *above* the previous Lower High. 

This signifies that the sellers are losing power, and the buyers have taken over. The bearish structure is broken, and a new uptrend may be beginning.

**Rule of Thumb:**
- In an Uptrend, you should only look for **Buy** opportunities.
- In a Downtrend, you should only look for **Sell** opportunities.
- In Consolidation, you should sit on your hands and wait.

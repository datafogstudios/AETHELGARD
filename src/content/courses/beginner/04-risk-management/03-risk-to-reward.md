## The Win Rate Illusion

Because of school and everyday life, we are conditioned to believe that to be "successful", we need to get a 90% or 100% on a test. 

In Forex trading, striving for a 90% win rate is not only incredibly difficult, it is often financially dangerous. How can a high win rate be dangerous? Because of **Risk to Reward**.

Many beginners boast a 90% win rate. They risk $100 on a trade, but as soon as they are up $5 in profit, they get scared and close the trade.
- They win 9 trades in a row (+$45). They feel like geniuses.
- On the 10th trade, it hits their stop loss (-$100).
- Despite winning 90% of the time, they are down -$55.

This is an **Inverse Risk to Reward Ratio**, and it is guaranteed to blow your account eventually.

## What is Risk to Reward (R:R)?

Risk to Reward (R:R) is simply the ratio of how much you are risking compared to how much you are trying to make.

It is calculated by comparing the exact distance to your Stop Loss vs the exact distance to your Take Profit.

### The 1:2 Ratio (The Holy Grail)

Professional traders almost never take a trade unless the setup offers a minimum of a **1:2 Risk to Reward Ratio**.
- **Risk:** 1 Unit
- **Reward:** 2 Units

If your Stop Loss is 20 pips away (risking $50), your Take Profit MUST be *at least* 40 pips away (targeting $100).

### The Math of a 1:2 R:R

Let's look at what happens if you take 10 trades, using a strict 1:2 R:R, risking $50 to make $100.

**Scenario:** You are a terrible trader. You lose 6 out of 10 trades.
- You lose 6 trades: - $300
- You win 4 trades: + $400
- **Net Profit:** + $100

*Even with a miserable 40% win rate, you made money.*

If you use a 1:3 R:R, you can mathematically lose 7 out of 10 trades and still be highly profitable. Professional traders do not care about their win rate. They care strictly about protecting their R:R ratio.

## Don't Force the Ratio

A common trap is forcing a ratio where it doesn't belong.

If you spot a great setup, but the next logical Resistance ceiling (Take Profit) is only 15 pips away, and your Stop Loss needs to be 30 pips away... **you do not take the trade.**

The R:R on that trade is 2:1 (risking $2 to make $1). You don't try to stretch your Take Profit past Resistance just to get a 1:2 ratio. You simply accept that the market isn't giving you a mathematical edge right now, and you walk away. 

> "There is always another trade tomorrow."

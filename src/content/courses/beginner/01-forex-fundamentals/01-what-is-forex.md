## The Global Marketplace

The **Foreign Exchange market**, commonly known as Forex or FX, is the global marketplace for trading national currencies against one another. 

Unlike stock markets, which have central exchanges (like the NYSE), Forex is an **Over-The-Counter (OTC)** market. This means trades take place electronically directly between parties all over the world, 24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

With an estimated daily trading volume exceeding **$7 trillion**, it is by far the largest and most liquid financial market in the world. To put that into perspective, the entire US stock market transacts roughly $200 billion a day.

### How it Works: The Currency Pair

In Forex, you are never just buying or selling a single entity. You are always trading one currency *against* another. This forms a **Currency Pair**.

When you look at a pair like `EUR/USD`:
- The **EUR** (Euro) is the **Base Currency**.
- The **USD** (US Dollar) is the **Quote Currency**.

If the `EUR/USD` exchange rate is **1.1050**, it means that exactly **1 Euro** is worth **1.1050 US Dollars**.

If you believe the Euro will strengthen against the US Dollar, you would **Buy** (Go Long) on EUR/USD. If you believe the Euro will weaken (or the Dollar will strengthen), you would **Sell** (Go Short) on EUR/USD.

### Majors, Minors, and Exotics

Currency pairs are broken down into three main categories based on volume and liquidity:

1. **The Majors:** These always include the US Dollar (USD) paired with another major global currency. Examples: `EUR/USD`, `GBP/USD`, `USD/JPY`, `USD/CHF`. These pairs have the lowest trading costs (spreads) due to their massive liquidity.
2. **The Minors (Crosses):** These pairs contain major currencies but *do not* include the US Dollar. Examples: `EUR/GBP`, `EUR/JPY`, `GBP/JPY`.
3. **The Exotics:** These pair a major currency with the currency of a developing or emerging economy. Examples: `USD/TRY` (Turkish Lira), `USD/ZAR` (South African Rand). They tend to be highly volatile and expensive to trade.

### Why Do Retail Traders Fail?

Most retail traders enter the Forex market drawn by the allure of high leverage and quick profits. However, they are competing directly against algorithms, international banks, and institutional hedge funds.

To survive, you must stop trading like a retail speculator and start thinking in terms of **liquidity** and **institutional order flow**. You must learn calculating proper [[Leverage|position sizes]] and defending your capital.

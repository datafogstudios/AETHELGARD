## The 24-Hour Cycle

A common trap for new traders is hearing that Forex is a "24-hour market" and assuming this means they can (or should) trade at any given hour. This is absolutely false. 

While you *can* push the buy or sell button at 2:00 AM your local time, the market operates through four major geographic hubs. **Volume, volatility, and institutional order flow are heavily concentrated into specific windows of time.** 

Time is just as important as price. A flawless technical setup executed at the wrong time of day will often fail.

## The Four Major Sessions

The trading day officially starts in Australasia and moves continuously across the globe:

### 1. The Sydney Session
This is the official start of the trading day. While the smallest of the mega-markets, it sets the initial tone for the Asian session. Volatility is generally very low.

### 2. The Tokyo (Asian) Session
Japan is the third-largest Forex trading center in the world. 
- **Characteristics:** The Asian session is notorious for **consolidation**. Prices often move within tight ranges as there is remarkably less liquidity than the European sessions. 
- **Strategy Note:** Smart money algorithms often use this consolidation range to build [[Liquidity|liquidity pools]] above and below the Asian range, which are often swept when London opens.

### 3. The London Session
London is the undisputed king of Forex, accounting for roughly 30% of all global volume.
- **Characteristics:** The London open introduces massive volatility. Significant trends for the day are almost always born during the London session.
- **Strategy Note:** Often, the London open will create a false macro move establishing the high or low of the day, specifically hunting the stops built up during the Asian session.

### 4. The New York Session
New York comes online just as London is heading to lunch. The US Dollar is involved in 85% of all Forex trades, making the US session incredibly influential.
- **Characteristics:** High volatility upon the open, often driven by US economic news releases (like NFP or CPI data) at 8:30 AM EST.

## Institutional Killzones

Smart money algorithms fire the hardest when liquidity is thickest. These periods of extreme volume happen when two major sessions **overlap**.

The most critical window of the entire trading day is the **London / New York Overlap** (typically 8:00 AM EST to 12:00 PM EST).

During this 4-hour window, the titans of Europe and the United States are both awake, at their desks, and transacting billions. 

### Why You Need to Know This
As a retail trader, you want to hitch a ride on institutional volume. Trading in the "dead zones" (late afternoon US time, before Tokyo begins) leaves you susceptible to wide spreads, algorithmic whipsaws, and flatline charts.

Identify these **Killzones**—the 2 to 3 hour windows around the London Open and the New York Open—and build your trading plan around them.

## The Food Chain of Forex

Unlike the stock market, where all participants trade through a centralized exchange and can see the same Level II data, Forex is decentralized. Because there is no single counter-party, trading occurs in a **hierarchical structure**.

Understanding this hierarchy is critical. When you push the "Buy" button, your order does not go into a magically fair, egalitarian pool. You are swimming in an ocean dominated by massive predators.

### Tier 1: The Interbank Market (The Apex Predators)

At the very top of the hierarchy sits the **Interbank Market**. This is composed of the world's largest banks (e.g., JPMorgan Chase, Citigroup, UBS, Deutsche Bank) and the **Central Banks** (e.g., the Federal Reserve, the European Central Bank).

1. **Central Banks:** They do not trade for profit. They trade to stabilize their national economy. When a central bank adjusts interest rates or intervenes in the market by printing or buying currency, they create macro-trends that last for months or years.
2. **Major Banks:** They trade massively on behalf of multinational corporations (commercial needs) and for their own proprietary speculation. **They dictate the prices.** The exchange rates you see on your screen are derived from the rates these tier-1 banks charge each other.

### Tier 2: Hedge Funds and Institutional Investors

Just below the major banks are massive hedge funds, pension funds, and multinational corporations. 
- A multi-national corporation like Apple might need to convert billions of US Dollars into Euros to build a factory in Germany. 
- A hedge fund might deploy algorithmic bots equipped with billions in capital to aggressively hunt [[Liquidity|liquidity pools]].

These institutions have direct access to Tier 1 pricing. Their sheer volume allows them to influence price and create short-term trends.

### Tier 3: Retail Market Makers (The Brokers)

Since individual traders do not have the millions required to access the Interbank market or the credit lines to trade directly with JPMorgan, they must go through a **Retail Broker**.

Your broker buys liquidity from Tier 1 and Tier 2 providers, adds a mark-up (the [[Spread|spread]]), and sells it to you. In many cases, specifically with "B-Book" brokers, the broker actually takes the opposite side of your trade. If you lose money, the broker profits directly.

### Tier 4: The Retail Trader (Plankton)

At the absolute bottom of the food chain is you—the retail speculator.

Retail traders account for less than **6%** of the daily volume in the Forex market. You have:
- The slowest execution speeds.
- The highest transaction costs (spreads + commissions).
- Zero ability to move or influence the price of an asset, even with maximum leverage.

### Why Does This Matter?

Most retail traders fail because they believe their technical indicators (like the RSI or a Moving Average crossover) predict the future. 

**Indicators do not move price. Institutional order flow moves price.**

The goal of this academy is *not* to teach you how to fight the Interbank market. The goal is to teach you how to track their footprints on the chart and ride their coattails. 

When you understand the hierarchy, you stop trading like Tier 4 plankton, and start trading in alignment with Tier 1 algorithms.
